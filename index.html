About Me

I am an undergraduate in Boston College's Gabelli Presidential Scholars Program studying computer science and mathematics, interested and experienced in software engineering and educational technology.


I am continually fine-tuning two main projects: Jamliterate, a web application that automatically generates well-grounded flashcards based on any text, and the David Game, a multiplayer drawing, guessing, and storytelling game available for download on the Apple App Store.


I work as a part-time software engineer at NeuroLyze, where I lead iOS app development and research methodologies of processing sports-related impact data to provide athletes and coaches with granular insight on concussion risk. I also work as a part-time teaching assistant in the computer science department at BC, assisting in Data Structures (last semester) and CS I (currently).
